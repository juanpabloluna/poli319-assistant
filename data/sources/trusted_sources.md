# Trusted Data Sources for POLI 319 Textbook Addendum

Use this guide to find reliable data for your assignment. For Formats 1 and 2 (table/graph updates), use the **same source as the textbook** wherever possible. If you switch sources, explain why and show the series are comparable (same country coverage, same methodology).

## Democracy and Political Regimes

**V-Dem (Varieties of Democracy)**
- URL: https://v-dem.net/data/
- What it covers: Annual democracy indices for all countries since 1789. Key indices: Liberal Democracy Index (LDI), Electoral Democracy Index (EDI), Participatory Democracy Index.
- Textbook connection: Democratic quality figures in Chapter 5 and the comparative regime chapter.
- How to cite: "V-Dem Country–Year Dataset v[version]" + access date.
- Access: Free (registration required for full download).
- Tip: The V-Dem Data Download tool lets you select countries and variables.

**Freedom House — Freedom in the World**
- URL: https://freedomhouse.org/report/freedom-world
- What it covers: Annual civil liberties and political rights scores (1–7 scale) for all countries.
- Textbook connection: Comparative democracy rankings.
- How to cite: Freedom House. [Year]. *Freedom in the World [Year]*. Washington, DC: Freedom House. [Access date].

**Polity5 / Polity Project**
- URL: https://www.systemicpeace.org/polityproject.html
- What it covers: Regime authority characteristics (-10 autocracy to +10 democracy) for all countries since 1800.
- Caution: Dataset updates ended in 2018. Use for historical series; use V-Dem for more recent data.

## Elections, Turnout, and Public Opinion

**IDEA Voter Turnout Database**
- URL: https://www.idea.int/data-tools/data/voter-turnout
- What it covers: Voter turnout by country and election type since 1945.
- How to cite: International IDEA. [Year]. *Voter Turnout Database*. Stockholm: International IDEA. [Access date].

**Latinobarometro**
- URL: https://www.latinobarometro.org/lat.jsp
- What it covers: Annual public opinion surveys in 18 Latin American countries. Key items: trust in institutions, support for democracy, satisfaction with democracy.
- Textbook connection: Public opinion data in democratic quality chapters.
- How to cite: Corporación Latinobarometro. [Year]. *Informe Latinobarometro [Year]*. Santiago. [Access date].
- Tip: Download the data file and use the codebook to find the exact question numbers.

**LAPOP — Latin American Public Opinion Project**
- URL: https://www.vanderbilt.edu/lapop/
- What it covers: Biannual surveys on political attitudes, crime victimization, trust in police, institutional legitimacy.
- How to cite: LAPOP. [Year]. *AmericasBarometer [Year]*. Vanderbilt University. [Access date].
- Tip: Use their online data analysis tool (LAPOP Insights) for quick cross-national comparisons.

## Inequality, Poverty, and Social Policy

**CEPAL / ECLAC — Social Panorama of Latin America**
- URL: https://www.cepal.org/en/publications (search "Social Panorama")
- What it covers: Annual poverty headcount, extreme poverty, and Gini coefficient for Latin American countries.
- Textbook connection: Primary source for poverty and inequality tables in Chapter 14.
- How to cite: ECLAC. [Year]. *Social Panorama of Latin America and the Caribbean [Year]*. Santiago: United Nations. [Access date].
- Important: Use the statistical annexes for country-level data.

**CEPALSTAT**
- URL: https://statistics.cepal.org/portal/cepalstat/
- What it covers: CEPAL's comprehensive online database — poverty, inequality, GDP, employment, social spending.
- Tip: Allows custom queries and data export. More flexible than the PDF reports.

**World Bank — World Development Indicators (WDI)**
- URL: https://data.worldbank.org/
- What it covers: GDP per capita, Gini coefficient, poverty headcount ratio, health, education indicators.
- How to cite: World Bank. [Year]. *World Development Indicators*. Washington, DC. [Access date].
- Important: The WDI Gini series uses different survey years per country. Check whether this is comparable to the textbook's original source before substituting.

**SEDLAC (Socio-Economic Database for Latin America and the Caribbean)**
- URL: https://www.cedlas.econo.unlp.edu.ar/wp/en/estadisticas/sedlac/
- What it covers: Harmonized household survey microdata for LAC countries, including Gini, poverty, labor markets.
- Recommended for: Researchers who want comparable cross-country inequality data with consistent methodology.

**UNDP Human Development Reports**
- URL: https://hdr.undp.org/data-center
- What it covers: Human Development Index (HDI), inequality-adjusted HDI, Gender Development Index.
- How to cite: UNDP. [Year]. *Human Development Report [Year]*. New York: UNDP. [Access date].

## Violence, Security, and Organized Crime

**UNODC — Global Study on Homicide**
- URL: https://www.unodc.org/unodc/en/data-and-analysis/homicide.html
- What it covers: Homicide rates per 100,000 population by country, disaggregated by sex and mechanism (firearm, non-firearm).
- How to cite: UNODC. [Year]. *Global Study on Homicide [Year]*. Vienna: UNODC. [Access date].
- Tip: The Excel data file is the most useful for country comparisons over time.

**InSight Crime — Annual Homicide Roundup**
- URL: https://insightcrime.org/
- What it covers: Investigative journalism on organized crime, drug trafficking, and violence in Latin America.
- Caution: This is journalistic analysis, NOT a primary statistical dataset. Use it to find primary sources and for qualitative context. Do not cite it as your source for numerical data.

**LAPOP — Crime Victimization Data**
- URL: https://www.vanderbilt.edu/lapop/ (same as above)
- Relevant questions: Victimization rates, fear of crime, trust in police, bribery experiences.

**Global Organized Crime Index**
- URL: https://ocindex.net/
- What it covers: Criminality and resilience scores for all countries. Useful for comparisons on criminal market activity.
- How to cite: GI-TOC. [Year]. *Global Organized Crime Index [Year]*. Geneva: GI-TOC. [Access date].

## State Capacity and Governance

**World Bank — Worldwide Governance Indicators (WGI)**
- URL: https://www.worldbank.org/en/publication/worldwide-governance-indicators
- What it covers: Six dimensions: Voice and Accountability, Political Stability, Government Effectiveness, Regulatory Quality, Rule of Law, Control of Corruption.
- How to cite: Kaufmann, D., Kraay, A., and Mastruzzi, M. [Year]. *Worldwide Governance Indicators*. World Bank. [Access date].

**ICTD/UNU-WIDER Government Revenue Dataset (GRD)**
- URL: https://www.wider.unu.edu/project/government-revenue-dataset
- What it covers: Tax revenue, non-tax revenue, grants as % of GDP for all countries since 1980.
- Textbook connection: State capacity and taxation discussions.
- How to cite: UNU-WIDER. [Year]. *Government Revenue Dataset*. Helsinki: UNU-WIDER. [Access date].

## General Rules for Using These Sources

1. **Cite the primary source**: For data from Our World in Data, Wikipedia, or news articles, always find and cite the underlying primary source (V-Dem, CEPAL, UNODC, etc.).

2. **Verify comparability**: For Formats 1 and 2, if you are updating a table or graph, use the same source the textbook used wherever possible. If not available, explicitly note the methodological differences.

3. **Include access date**: For all online datasets, include the date you accessed the data (required for APSA and Chicago citations).

4. **Note survey years**: For LAPOP and Latinobarometro, note which survey wave(s) you are using — the year of the report and the year of the survey can differ.

5. **Never fabricate**: If you cannot find reliable data for a specific country or year, say so. Do not estimate or extrapolate from other sources without clearly stating that you are doing so.

6. **AI cannot retrieve data**: The assistant can tell you which database to use and how to navigate it, but cannot browse the internet or download datasets on your behalf. You must access these sources directly.
